# Priority Classification & Recommended Actions

This guide explains how the dashboard classifies reporting intensity
across U.S. states using CRDC school-level bullying data. The
classifications are **decision-support indicators** designed to help
identify jurisdictions that may warrant additional review. They should
**not** be interpreted as rankings of school quality or definitive
evidence of bullying prevalence.

---

🔴 High Priority

Criteria

≥ 2.00 reported allegations per school

Interpretation

States in this category report substantially more bullying allegations per school than the national average. Higher reporting intensity may indicate elevated bullying prevalence, stronger reporting practices, or both. These jurisdictions should be prioritized for further investigation rather than immediate conclusions.

Example Actions

These actions could include:

- Conduct a district-level review to identify schools with consistently high allegation rates.
- Allocate additional bullying prevention resources, such as staff training or student awareness programs.
- Review whether specific student groups or allegation types are disproportionately affected.
- Compare current reporting trends with previous CRDC reporting periods.
- Work with school administrators to evaluate existing intervention strategies.
- Monitor whether implemented prevention initiatives reduce reporting intensity over time.

Example

A state averaging 2.70 allegations per school would be classified as High Priority because reported incidents occur substantially more frequently than the national baseline.

⸻

🟠 Medium Priority

Criteria

1.00–1.99 reported allegations per school

Interpretation

States in this range show moderate reporting intensity. They do not require immediate intervention but should be monitored to identify emerging trends before they become persistent.

Example Actions

These actions could include:

- Compare reporting patterns across districts.
- Identify districts with increasing allegation trends.
- Review whether prevention programs are producing measurable improvements.
- Monitor annual changes in reporting intensity.
- Evaluate whether additional staff guidance or policy reviews may be beneficial.

Example

A state averaging 1.45 allegations per school would continue to be monitored and compared against future reporting cycles.

⸻

🟢 Routine Monitoring

Criteria

Below 1.00 reported allegation per school

Interpretation

Reporting intensity is comparatively low within the available dataset. Routine monitoring should continue to ensure reporting patterns remain stable over time.

Example Actions

These actions could include:

- Continue annual monitoring through future CRDC collections.
- Compare long-term reporting trends.
- Maintain existing bullying prevention initiatives.
- Review any isolated districts showing unusual increases.
- Share effective prevention practices across schools where appropriate.

Example

A state averaging 0.82 allegations per school would remain under routine monitoring.

⸻

⚪ No Reported Allegations

Criteria

0 reported allegations

Interpretation

No allegations were reported during the selected reporting period. This should not automatically be interpreted as evidence that bullying did not occur, as reporting practices and data completeness may vary.

Example Actions

These actions could include:

- Review whether all schools submitted complete CRDC data.
- Verify reporting procedures with school districts.
- Assess whether underreporting may be influencing results.
- Continue routine monitoring in future reporting cycles.
- Compare with neighboring jurisdictions and previous reporting periods.

Example

A state reporting 0 allegations should first be reviewed for reporting completeness before drawing conclusions.

⸻

Additional Decision Rule – Disciplinary Response Ratio

Calculation

Students Receiving Disciplinary Action ÷ Total Reported Allegations

Threshold

Ratio < 0.75

Interpretation

A relatively small proportion of reported allegations resulted in documented disciplinary actions. This does not necessarily indicate ineffective school responses, but it may suggest differences in documentation practices, disciplinary policies, or reporting consistency.

Example Actions

These actions could include:

- Review whether disciplinary actions are being consistently documented.
- Compare disciplinary practices across school districts.
- Assess whether school policies are applied consistently.
- Review whether alternative interventions are being used instead of formal disciplinary actions.
- Identify schools with unusually low response ratios for further investigation.

Example

A ratio of 0.60 indicates that approximately 60 disciplinary actions were documented for every 100 reported allegations, suggesting that documentation or response practices may warrant additional review.
